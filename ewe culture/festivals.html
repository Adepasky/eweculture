Celebrating Our Heritage

Ewe festivals are vibrant expressions of our rich cultural heritage, blending history, spirituality, music, dance, and communal celebration. These annual events bring together Ewe communities from Ghana, Togo, and Benin to honor our ancestors, celebrate harvests, and preserve our traditions for future generations.

November

Hogbetsotso Festival

Anlo Land, Volta Region

The most famous Ewe festival commemorating the migration from Notsie in present-day Togo. Features purification ceremonies, drumming, dancing, and a grand durbar of chiefs.

Learn More
August

Asafotu Festival

Ada, Greater Accra

A warrior festival celebrated by the Ada people featuring mock battles, military displays, and rituals to honor ancestors and commemorate victories in past wars.

Learn More
September

Yam Festival (Te Za)

Throughout Ewe Land

A harvest festival to thank the gods for bounty and to purify the people for the new year. Features the offering of new yams, feasting, and traditional dances.

Learn More
March

Dzawuwu Festival

Dzodze, Volta Region

A planting festival marking the beginning of the farming season with rituals to ensure good harvests, featuring traditional music and dance performances.

Learn More
July

Glimetoto Festival

Peki, Volta Region

A unique festival featuring acrobatic displays and warrior dances, originally performed to prepare young men for battle and now preserved as cultural heritage.

Learn More
December

Sasadu Festival

Some, Volta Region

A thanksgiving festival celebrated by the Some people featuring communal feasting, traditional games, and performances of the "Sasadu" dance.

Learn More

Festival Highlights

Agbadza Dance at Hogbetsotso

The rhythmic Agbadza dance performed during Hogbetsotso, with dancers moving in perfect synchronization to the drum beats.

Durbar of Chiefs

The grand procession of Ewe chiefs in their colorful regalia during festival celebrations, showcasing the rich traditional leadership.

Traditional Rituals

Ancient purification ceremonies performed by priests and priestesses to honor ancestors and seek blessings for the community.
